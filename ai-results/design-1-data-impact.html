The Crisis

A population growing faster than we can help

There are 23,053 street cats in Varna, and the municipality estimates the population has been growing by 85% every 2 years. There are not enough care-takers and resources for so many cats. Cats are dying of viruses, poisoning, and traffic accidents every day.

In 2025, the council approved a budget for around 800 cats — and more than 1,000 were neutered. But this isn't enough to stop population growth. Significant additional measures are required.

Legal Basis

The law is on our side

Under Art. 56, Art. 40(3) and Art. 40a(1) of the Animal Protection Act, when an increase in street cat populations is observed, municipal councils must introduce mass neutering programmes to achieve "management and reduction" of the population.

Every single vet we surveyed across Varna supports this programme. The science, the law, and the public are aligned.
