Why this matters

The data, the law, and the experts all point to the same solution: a municipal mass neutering programme.

85%

Population surge every 2 years

The street cat population in Varna is growing exponentially. With more than 23,000 cats today, the situation is a humanitarian crisis for animals.

24/24

Vets support the programme

We surveyed 24 veterinary clinics in Varna Municipality. Every single vet stated they support a municipal mass neutering programme.

Law

It's legally required

Under Art. 40a(1) of the Animal Protection Act, municipal councils must introduce mass neutering programmes when populations increase.

Our Story

For Leo — and all the others

LeoLife Bulgaria was born in July 2023 when we met Leo: a 4-month-old kitten stumbling alone by the Armenian Church in Varna's old town. He had hydrocephalus — water in the brain — and was exhausted and disoriented. We stayed with him through the night.

A few months later, Leo passed away from his illness. But his story sparked a fire. The cat in our logo is an outline of Leo's head. This fight is for him, and for the thousands of cats like him.
