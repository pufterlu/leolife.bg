The crisis

Why we need mass neutering — now

There are 23,053 street cats in Varna. Without intervention, the population will exceed 40,000 within two years. Cats are dying of viruses, poisoning, and traffic accidents every day. There are simply not enough care-takers and resources.

In January 2026, a first campaign neutered over 1,000 cats — a great start, but not enough to stop the population from growing. We need a sustained, funded municipal programme.

Read about the 2025 campaign →

⚖️

It's the law

Under Art. 40a(1) of the Animal Protection Act, municipalities must introduce mass neutering when populations are observed to be growing.

🩺

Vets agree unanimously

We surveyed 24 veterinary clinics across Varna Municipality — every single vet supports the programme.

🌍

Internationally proven

TNR (trap-neuter-return) is internationally recognized and proven to reduce street cat populations humanely and effectively.

🐾

Better lives for cats

Studies show neutered cats live significantly longer and healthier lives than intact street cats.

In memory of Leo

The cat in our logo is an outline of Leo's head — the kitten whose story started it all. LeoLife Bulgaria is for Leo, and for all the cats like him.

Our story

How it all began

In July 2023, we met Leo: a 4-month-old kitten stumbling alone by the Armenian Church in Varna's old town. He had hydrocephalus — water in the brain — and was exhausted, disoriented, and completely alone. We stayed with him through the night.

A vet offered to treat him, and if he survived, to find him a home in Germany. We were so relieved. But a few months later, Leo passed away from his illness. His story sparked a fire in us — a fire of sadness, anger, hope, and determination.

Since then, we have helped Nadya, Nelly, Lulu, Lea, Sia, Sammy, Freddy, Lily, Stefan, Gabi, Mickey, Tommy, Simeon, Max, Sienna, and more. Creatures that through no fault of their own ended up suffering on the streets.
