Cats are dying.
The law is on our side.

There are 23,053 street cats in Varna and the municipality estimates the population has been growing by 85% every 2 years. Without a mass neutering programme, we will have more than 40,000 cats within 2 years.

Cats suffer and die from viruses, poisoning, and traffic accidents every day. There are not enough caretakers or resources to support so many animals. This is a street cat crisis.

In January 2026, the first campaign launched and neutered over 1,000 cats. A great start — but far short of what is needed to reverse population growth.

  • Under Art. 40a(1) of the Animal Protection Act, Varna Municipality is legally required to run a mass neutering programme.
  • Every single one of the 24 veterinary clinics we surveyed supports the programme.
  • TNR (trap-neuter-return) is an internationally recognised method proven to reduce street cat populations.
  • Neutered cats live significantly longer than intact street cats (Nutter, 2005).
  • There are only 2 options: mass euthanasia (illegal & inhumane) or mass neutering (legal & humane).

Civil resistance works.

In 2025, after years of advocacy, Varna Municipal Council approved a budget for a free street cat neutering campaign. Citizen demand was so high the budget was spent within 3 months — over 1,000 cats neutered.

We assisted the municipality in developing the plan and provided technical assistance to vets applying for the programme.
