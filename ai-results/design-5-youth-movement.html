The case for mass neutering

⚖️

The Law Requires It

Under Art. 40a(1) of the Animal Protection Act, when street cat populations are observed to be growing, municipal councils must introduce mass neutering programmes. This is not a suggestion — it's a legal obligation.

🩺

Every Vet Agrees

We surveyed 24 veterinary clinics in Varna Municipality. The result: every single vet supports a municipal mass neutering programme. The scientific consensus is unanimous.

🌍

Proven Around the World

TNR (trap-neuter-return) is internationally recognized as the humane, effective method for managing street cat populations. Studies show neutered cats live significantly longer lives.

🐾

This Is a Crisis

Cats are dying of viruses, poisoning, and traffic accidents every day. Growing from 2,008 to 23,053 in 8 years — the population will exceed 40,000 within 2 years without intervention.

// our story

For Leo — the kitten who started it all

In July 2023, we met Leo: a 4-month-old kitten stumbling alone by the Armenian Church in Varna's old town. He had hydrocephalus — water in the brain. We stayed with him through the night and got him to a vet. A few months later, Leo passed away. His story sparked a fire in us.

The cat outline in our logo is Leo's head. LeoLife Bulgaria is for him — and for the thousands of cats like him who were born into a world that wasn't ready for them.
