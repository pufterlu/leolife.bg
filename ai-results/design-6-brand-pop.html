The case for action

Why a municipal mass neutering programme?

The street cat population in Varna is growing exponentially. Without sustained municipal action, we will have more than 40,000 cats within 2 years — and the suffering will continue.

In January 2026, the first campaign launched and neutered over 1,000 cats. A fantastic start, but not enough to reverse growth on its own. Read about our 2025 work →

⚖️

It's legally required

Art. 40a(1) of the Animal Protection Act requires municipalities to introduce mass neutering when populations grow.

🩺

All 24 vets agree

We surveyed every veterinary clinic in Varna Municipality — every single one supports the programme.

🌍

Proven internationally

TNR (trap-neuter-return) is internationally recognized and proven to humanely reduce street cat populations.

💚

Better lives for cats

Neutered cats live significantly longer and healthier lives. It's the humane choice.

For Leo

The cat in our logo is an outline of Leo's head — the kitten whose story started it all. This fight is for him, and for all the cats like him.

Our story

From one kitten to a city-wide campaign

In July 2023, we met Leo: a 4-month-old kitten stumbling alone by the Armenian Church in Varna's old town. He had hydrocephalus — water in the brain — and was exhausted and completely alone. We stayed with him through the night.

A vet offered to care for him, but a few months later, Leo passed away. His story sparked a fire — a fire of sadness, anger, and determination to fight for the thousands of cats like him.

Since then, we've helped Nadya, Nelly, Lulu, Lea, Sia, Sammy, Freddy, Lily, Stefan, Gabi, Mickey, Tommy, Simeon, Max, Sienna, and many more. Read our full story →
